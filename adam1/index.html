A Tale of Ascena (Example)

Below is an example of the kind of adventure you can expect in the realm of Ascena—lighthearted, wacky, and heartwarming. This is not the final tale, but a playful glimpse into the spirit of our world: maximum adventure for minimum wage.

Chapter I: Hired for a Hoot

In the bustling realm of Ascena, where tavern bards strum tunes of glory and the sun dips low with a golden wink, there’s a little-known crew of adventurers who’ll take on any quest—for the right price, or just a hearty meal. Meet the Motley Misfits, a ragtag band of dreamers led by Pippa Pockets, a self-proclaimed ‘treasure consultant’ with a knack for finding trouble. Her companions? Grumble, a dwarf baker who wields a rolling pin like a warhammer; Lila Lute, a bard whose songs accidentally summon critters; and Thistle, a hedgehog-turned-human (long story) who’s just happy to be here.

One soggy morning in the village of Dampwick, a desperate farmer named Old Man Turnip hobbled into the Rusty Tankard with a plea—and a measly three coppers. His prize turnip, the ‘Giggle Gourd,’ had vanished, and with it, the village’s only chance at winning the Annual Harvest Hootenanny. The Misfits, down to their last stale biscuit, took the job. ‘Maximum adventure, minimum wage!’ Pippa cheered, as Grumble muttered about needing yeast more than heroics.

Chapter II: The Trail of Giggles

The trail led to the Whispering Woods, where trees gossip louder than tavern wenches. Lila strummed a ditty to calm the spooked party, only to accidentally summon a flock of disco-dancing squirrels. ‘Not again!’ groaned Thistle, dodging a nut tossed with rhythm. The squirrels, however, pointed the way with twitchy tails—toward a muddy clearing where the Giggle Gourd sat, inexplicably giggling. Guarding it? A gang of mischievous bog sprites, tiny critters with mossy mohawks, who’d stolen the turnip to host their own ‘Bog Boogie Bash.’

Pippa, ever the negotiator, offered a trade: Grumble’s stale biscuit for the gourd. The sprites, delighted by the crumbly ‘delicacy,’ agreed—but only if the Misfits joined their dance-off. What followed was a hilarious flop of flailing limbs—Grumble’s dwarf jig, Lila’s lute-twirl, and Thistle’s hedgehog-inspired shuffle won the sprites’ hearts (and a few muddy hugs). With the gourd giggling under Pippa’s arm, they trudged back, covered in swamp glitter but grinning ear to ear.

Chapter III: Turnip Triumph

Back in Dampwick, the Harvest Hootenanny was in full swing—fiddles fiddling, pies flying, and villagers eyeing the prize podium. Old Man Turnip wept as he reunited with his Giggle Gourd, which let out a particularly loud chortle during the judging. The Misfits, still swampy, were hailed as heroes of the harvest. Their prize? A single shiny turnip trophy—and a lifetime supply of turnip stew. ‘Best wage yet!’ Pippa winked, as Grumble sighed, dreaming of bread instead.

That night, under a lantern-lit sky, the village danced till dawn. Lila’s lute (carefully tuned to avoid squirrel summons) played a merry jig, Thistle taught the kids a hedgehog hop, and even Grumble cracked a smile, passing out turnip tarts he’d whipped up on the spot. The Motley Misfits, broke as ever, felt richer than kings. In Ascena, they learned, adventure doesn’t need gold—just a good laugh and better friends.

Chapter IV: The Next Odd Job

As the Hootenanny wound down, a frantic messenger stumbled in, clutching a soggy parchment. ‘Help! My prize goose lays eggs of pure chaos!’ he wailed, offering two bent spoons as payment. Pippa’s eyes gleamed—another quest for the books. ‘Pack the stew, crew! We’ve got a fowl problem!’ she rallied. Grumble grumbled (of course), Lila tuned up for goose-charming, and Thistle just hoped no one would turn him back into a hedgehog. Off they went, into the quirky wilds of Ascena, where every odd job promised maximum adventure for minimum wage—and a story worth more than any treasure.

This is but one tale of the realm, a preview of the heart and humor woven into our world. The final epic of the Dying Daylight awaits, but know that in Ascena, even the smallest heroes can spark the biggest laughs—and the warmest bonds.

Cast & Crew

The Making of an Epic

Creating the world of Ascena required a perfect blend of practical effects and cutting-edge technology. Our team of artisans spent months crafting authentic medieval props and costumes, while our cinematographers developed new techniques to capture the ethereal quality of the Dying Daylight.

The film features over 15 original musical compositions performed by a 60-piece orchestra, recorded in ancient cathedrals to capture the perfect acoustics. Every sword clash, every spell cast, and every whispered prophecy was meticulously designed to transport audiences into our medieval fantasy realm.

  • Production Designer: Kai Worldbuilder - Created Middle Earth for TV

Director's Vision

Adam Newman

Director • Producer • Writer

"Tales of the Dying Daylight is more than a fantasy epic—it's a meditation on the duality that exists within us all. We live in the twilight between our dreams and fears, between who we are and who we wish to become."

Adam Newman is a filmmaker whose recent work spans narrative shorts, features, and series development. His credits include directing and producing projects such as Round the Decay (2025), Everwinter Night (2023), Carolina Shag (2023), Ice Patrol (2019), Nomad (2019), and Scarlet Cay (2018).

Tales of the Dying Daylight is currently in pre‑production, with Newman attached as Director and Producer. His approach blends handmade craft with modern technique—drawing on medieval illuminated manuscripts, Pre‑Raphaelite palettes, and contemporary cinematic language to balance light and shadow.

The Philosophy Behind the Film

"I wanted to explore what happens when the boundaries we take for granted begin to dissolve. The dying daylight is that liminal moment—neither day nor night—where transformation is possible. It's in these in-between spaces where we discover who we truly are."

"Fantasy allows us to externalize our internal struggles. The Shadow Lord isn't just a villain; he's the embodiment of our collective fear of change. Lyra's journey isn't just about saving the world; it's about accepting that sometimes, the world needs to change."
